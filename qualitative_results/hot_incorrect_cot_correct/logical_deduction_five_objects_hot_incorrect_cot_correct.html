In an antique car show, there are five vehicles: a convertible, a sedan, a tractor, a minivan, and a limousine. The tractor is newer than the minivan. The tractor is older than the limousine. The convertible is older than the sedan. The convertible is the second-newest. Options: (A) The convertible is the second-oldest (B) The sedan is the second-oldest (C) The tractor is the second-oldest (D) The minivan is the second-oldest (E) The limousine is the second-oldest

Answer:Here's how we can determine the order of the vehicles from oldest to newest: 1. **The convertible is the second-newest:** This tells us the convertible is fourth in age (out of five vehicles). 2. **The convertible is older than the sedan:** This means the sedan is newer than the convertible, making the sedan the newest (fifth in age). 3. **The tractor is newer than the minivan:** The minivan is older than the tractor. 4. **The tractor is older than the limousine:** The limousine is newer than the tractor. Combining these facts, we know: * Sedan (Newest) * Convertible * Limousine * Tractor * Minivan (Oldest) The question asks for the second-oldest vehicle, which is the tractor. Answer: {C}
